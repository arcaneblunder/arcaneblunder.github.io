Experience the Horror and Suspense

The Idaho Horror Film Festival, Idaho's first and longest-running horror film festival, returns for its 8th edition October 7-10, 2021. Every year, the festival attracts the latest and best genre films from around the world and attendees from all over the country for an incredible gathering of film fans in beautiful Downtown Boise, Idaho. Experience four packed days, with an eclectic mix of horror, suspense, thriller, dark fantasy, sci-fi, and dark comedy in Boise's historic (and haunted) Egyptian Theatre.

The festival line-up offers more than 60 short films, special programs, guests, panels, and events. If you love genre films, then you can't miss this fest. The 2021 Idaho Horror Film Festival will honor Trailblazer Award goes to Director Chelsea Stardust. She will be honored after the screening of her feature film, SATANIC PANIC.

In addition to the films, the Idaho Horror Film Fest puts on fun events including the Downtown Bar Walk, Horror Poetry Slam, Flash Fiction Competition, and a Horror Trivia Night. We even have our own beer, Draculager, made with love by friends at Woodland Empire Ale Craft.

Announcements

The Idaho Horror Film Festival is pleased to announce that we have been awarded The Department of Arts & History grant for Fiscal Year 2020. Grants are reviewed by a panel and awarded based on quality, management, community accessibility, and budget—supporting projects that ultimately contribute toward making Boise a lasting, innovative, and vibrant city through arts and history. We are honored to be in this group of esteemed colleagues and we are excited to be a part of Boise's thriving arts culture.

Featured Film

Satanic Panic

Directed by: Chelsea Stardust

Runtime: 90:00

Sam’s first day as a pizza delivery driver is not going according to plan. At the end of a long day and not enough tips, her last delivery turns out to be for a group of Satanists looking for someone to sacrifice. Now in a fight for her life, Sam must fend off witches, evil spells and demonic creatures, all while trying to keep her body – and soul – intact.
